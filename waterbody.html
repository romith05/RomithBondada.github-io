WATER BODY DETECTION IN SATELLITE IMAGES USING DEEP LEARNING

This project uses machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) techniques to provide a novel approach to water body recognition. The suggested technology automatically and efficiently detects and delineates water bodies by utilizing high-resolution satellite imagery, remote sensing data, and sophisticated image processing algorithms. The main elements of this methodology include data preprocessing, feature extraction, and supervised machine learning. For feature extraction, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are utilized to capture the unique spectral and spatial properties of water bodies. The model is trained using a labelled dataset, which helps it understand and generalize patterns of water bodies. The study's findings show that the AI-ML strategy performs faster and more accurately than conventional techniques. Large datasets can be handled by the system, which can detect water bodies in real-time or almost real-time for a variety of applications. Moreover, the flexibility of the approach enables its application to diverse geographical locations and environmental circumstances.

EXISTING SYSTEM

The current technology increases the precision and effectiveness of water body detection in satellite imagery by utilising Convolutional Neural Networks, a deep learning technique. With a variety of satellite photos, the CNN will be built to automatically identify and learn from the peculiarities of water bodies.

PRPOSED SYSTEM

FCNs revolutionized the field of semantic segmentation by enabling end-to-end learning for pixel-wise classification, as opposed to earlier methods that used hand-crafted features and post-processing techniques. They have been widely used in applications like autonomous driving, medical image analysis, and more, where precise pixel-level understanding of an image is crucial. Various FCN architectures, such as FCN-32s, FCN-16s, and FCN-8s, have been developed to improve segmentation accuracy and efficiency.

Fully Convolutional Networks, or FCNs, are an architecture used mainly for semantic segmentation. They employ solely locally connected layers, such as convolution, pooling and upsampling. Avoiding the use of dense layers means less parameters (making the networks faster to train). A fully convolutional neural network (FCN) is a special type of CNN that only contains convolutional layers As a result of using only convolutional layers, FCNs can work with input images of any size, while standard CNNs only accept fixed-size images. FCN classifies the pixels, so as to achieve the level of image semantic segmentation. Different from the classical convolutional neural networks (CNN), FCN uses convolution layers instead of the fully connected layers. So it can accept image of arbitrary size.
